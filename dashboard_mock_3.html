How to read · Negative (<0%) means bondholders are accepting less than expected short rates — usually QE or flight-to-safety doing the work. Low (0 to 0.5%) is benign. Normal (0.5–1.5%) is healthy compensation for duration risk. Stress (>1.5%) historically precedes fiscal crises (1970s repression, 1980s Volcker, 2008 GFC). Currently +0.42% — surprisingly low given $36T debt, suggesting hidden demand sources (regulatory captive bidders, stablecoin Treasury collateral) are absorbing the supply. Watch for expansion above 0.7% as scenario 1/3 confirmation.

How to read · Each component is z-scored on a 10-year rolling window then averaged. Negative readings (below zero) indicate easier-than-average financial conditions and tight credit spreads — the current state. § 3D

Bridge · how the scenarios feed the gold model

The gold model doesn't take scenarios as input — it operates on its own 5-driver score. But the two should agree most of the time. When they don't, that's information: the model is tracking gold's micro-environment while scenarios track macro. Disagreement means the macro story hasn't yet shown up in the indicator components, or vice versa.

How to read · Background shading is the macro scenario classifier's reading each month. Brass overlay is the gold model's actual position (filled = long, empty = cash). Agreement looks like brass-on-rust (S1 long) or brass-on-burgundy (S3 long) — the macro story justifies being in gold. Disagreement is when brass disappears during a teal (S2) period — the model is cash even though scenarios suggest gold isn't needed, which is the model doing its job. The most useful disagreements are when scenarios shift before the model does, or vice versa — that's the leading-indicator signal worth watching.
§ 04

Methodology & data sources

What this is

A 5-driver macro indicator that switches a tactical gold sleeve between 100% gold and 100% cash. Drivers: real yield Δ6M, DXY Δ12M, CPI YoY Δ12M, gold/SPX Δ12M, NFCI level. Each driver z-scored on a 60-month backward window, sign-adjusted so positive = bullish gold, lagged one month, equally weighted, smoothed over 6 months, classified into bull/neutral/bear at ±0.5.

Position rule is early-buy long-flat: enter long on bull, exit on bear, but enter long on the first neutral after a bear (catches the 10–20% post-bear bounce that pure bull-confirmation misses).

What it doesn't do

The strategy delivers its 2.2× wealth advantage by avoiding gold's worst bear markets — 1981–85, 1997–99, 2012–15. It does not generate alpha during bull markets; when gold trends, the strategy is just buy-and-hold. From December 2015 through March 2026 it has not flipped to cash even once.

Out-of-sample reliability should be considered moderate, not high. Roughly 10 major bear-to-bull transitions in 52 years.

Glossary

TIPS · Treasury Inflation-Protected Security. The yield is the market's real interest rate.

Term premium · the extra yield demanded for holding long bonds beyond expected average short rates. NY Fed ACM model.

Breakeven inflation · 10Y nominal − 10Y TIPS. Implied inflation expectation.

5y5y forward · expected inflation between years 5 and 10. The Fed's preferred long-run measure.

NFCI · Chicago Fed National Financial Conditions Index. Positive = stress.
